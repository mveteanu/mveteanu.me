From Early Coding to Technical Mastery

My journey in tech began at 13, fueled by curiosity and a ZX-Spectrum 48K clone (HC-90). Like many 8-bit computers of its time, it came with built-in BASIC, which I quickly outgrew as I set my sights on machine-level coding. I dove headfirst into Z80 assembly, spending countless nights coding, optimizing, and reverse-engineering games—laying the foundation for a lifelong fascination with low-level systems and performance optimization. See Atomix developed during this time.

Parallel to this, I gained early access to PCs and immersed myself in DOS, exploring executable file formats, FAT structures, and INT 21h interrupts. During this time, I founded my first "garage" software initiative—VMASOFT—writing DOS software in Pascal and x86 assembly. Many of these early creations still live in my legacy GitHub repository. Among them: graphics libraries like MCGA and windowing systems like VMAWin, and even exploratory work in virus behavior (all safely archived, of course).

From there, I moved into Windows development, mastering Delphi and building commercial products like PowerTests Desktop — a test assessment suite adopted by several schools. This project became a launchpad for my first entrepreneurial SaaS venture: PowerTests.NET, built using classic ASP and the IE 4.0 DOM model. I handled the full lifecycle—from architecture to marketing—and even published technical articles on emerging web technologies of the time.

Engineering Leadership in Enterprise Innovation

My entrepreneurial path evolved into enterprise-grade innovation when I joined STARLIMS (later acquired by Abbott). There, I became an early .NET adopter and helped architect the XFD Technology Platform, a model-driven development environment inspired by XAML and Microsoft's RAD tools. XFD enabled rapid delivery of complex LIMS workflows for Fortune 500 clients and government labs.

Over more than a decade at STARLIMS / Abbott, I held senior engineering leadership roles focused on technology and architecture. I drove multi-million-dollar platform initiatives that delivered enterprise-grade solutions for global clients and saved the company hundreds of millions through forward-looking architecture and strategy.

I led multiple product teams, mentored engineers across levels, and championed best practices in software architecture, security, and product lifecycle management.

I also remained deeply technical, dictating architectural direction using .NET, Azure/AWS cloud services, event-driven microservices, and emerging GenAI (LLM, RAG) technologies.

From building greenfield platforms to modernizing legacy systems, I consistently delivered scalable solutions that combined innovation, business impact, and engineering excellence.

Entrepreneurship in EdTech

After my tenure at STARLIMS / Abbott, I launched codeguppy.com, an EdTech SaaS platform designed to introduce young coders to JavaScript through a simplified online coding environment. I architected and developed the platform end-to-end, authored educational content, and grew its reach through a multi-channel presence on YouTube, Twitter, and Instagram. This venture allowed me to blend technical execution, product strategy, and community growth—transforming a passion for teaching into a scalable business that combined platform development with modern digital engagement.

I also established a strong thought leadership presence by publishing articles on AI integration, cloud-native architecture, and platform engineering, engaging an audience of 7K+ LinkedIn followers. I also provided consulting on GenAI strategy, advising clients on LLMs, RAG, MCP, and cloud-native architectures for scalable AI-enabled systems.
